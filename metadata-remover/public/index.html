Frequently Asked Questions

What is metadata?

Metadata is hidden information embedded in files. Photos contain GPS coordinates, camera settings, and dates. Documents store author names, company details, and revision history. This data can compromise your privacy.

Is it really private?

Yes! All processing happens directly in your web browser using JavaScript. Your files never leave your device and we cannot see, access, or store them.

How do I verify metadata was removed?

We show you exactly what was removed from each file. You can also use any metadata viewer tool to confirm your cleaned files contain no hidden information.

Is there a file size limit?

Since processing happens in your browser, the only limit is your device's capability. Most modern devices can handle files up to 100MB easily.
