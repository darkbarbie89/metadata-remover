How to Remove PDF Metadata (Author, Title, Keywords)

Updated October 3, 2025 • 5 min read

PDFs can leak more than you expect. Hidden properties like author, title, keywords, creator, and producer may reveal internal details. Here’s the simplest way to clear them before sharing.

Quick Steps

  1. Open MetaRemoval.
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Wait a moment while metadata is removed locally.
  4. Download the cleaned PDF.

Note: Encrypted or password-protected PDFs may not be editable without the password. MetaRemoval will mark them as “Protected”.

Clean a PDF Now

What Gets Removed?

  • Title, Author, Subject, Keywords
  • Creator, Producer
  • Embedded thumbnails and select auxiliary properties

Best Practices

  • Keep an original copy for your records.
  • After cleaning, open File → Properties in your PDF viewer to confirm fields are blank.
  • If the file is protected, export a new, unprotected version first (if you have rights and password), then clean.
